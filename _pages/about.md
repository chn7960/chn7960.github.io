I am a **Senior Researcher** at the [Korea Institute of Energy Research (KIER)](https://www.kier.re.kr/), based in the **Energy ICT Convergence Research Department**. My research sits at the intersection of **building energy**, **indoor environmental quality**, and **computer vision**, with the overarching goal of designing **occupant-centric control** systems that improve thermal comfort while reducing energy consumption in buildings.

I received my Ph.D. in Architectural Engineering from **Yonsei University** (AE Lab, advisor: Prof. Taeyeon Kim), where my dissertation — *Deep Vision-based Occupant-Centric Control for HVAC Systems* — laid the foundation for much of my current work.

Research Interests
======
- **Occupant-centric control** & human-building interaction
- **Deep learning / computer vision** for occupant information sensing (clothing insulation, metabolic rate, occupancy)
- **Grid-interactive Efficient Buildings (GEB)** and demand flexibility
- **Thermal comfort** modeling (PMV/PPD, MRT estimation)
- **Indoor air quality** and ventilation strategies

Current Focus
======
At KIER I serve as principal investigator on projects for autonomous building energy management and field-based evaluation of national energy-efficiency programs. I am actively developing practical control strategies that deliver demand flexibility without compromising occupant comfort, and exploring the role of large language models in building equipment maintenance.
